ABOUT OUR RESEARCH

The inquisitiveness of the human mind has been the driving force for advancements in all the fields. Yes, that is true, right from the stone age, the human race has evolved by developing new technologies that make life on the planet easier. One thing leads to another and here we are, developing artificially intelligent robots to assists us in fields like medical, disaster management, outer space exploration, car industry, military surveillance etc. In the current era, a large volume of remotely sensed images is accessible from various acquisition sources like satellites, manned and unnamed aerial vehicles. This research group is connected with the development of new techniques for better analysis of remotely sensed images so as to fulfil the aim of real-time earth monitoring. The primary interest of the group is to apply knowledge from machine learning, pattern recognition, etc, to remote sensing. The group specialises in artificial neural networks and has numerous publications in applying shallow and deep learning for remotely sensed image analysis. At present, we are working towards agriculture monitoring using adaptive crop-cover monitoring using remotely sensed aerial images.
